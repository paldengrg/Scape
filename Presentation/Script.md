**Target Audience:** Evaluators, Investors, and Community Members  
**Presenter:** Palden (Lead Developer)

## Slide 1: Title Slide — Welcome to SCAPE
* **Slide Title:** SCAPE: Rediscover Nature in Sydney's Bustling Metropolis
* **Visuals:** Full-bleed deep forest green background, striking white modern title "SCAPE", and sage green accent bars. Calming, premium, and clean.
* **Duration:** 1 minute

### Presenter Script:
> "Good morning, everyone. Welcome to the presentation of SCAPE. 
> 
> When you live in a bustling metropolis like Sydney, it is incredibly easy to get consumed by the concrete jungle—the constant noise, study stress, and work pressure. As human beings, we have an innate need to step outside, breathe fresh air, and connect with nature. However, finding the *right* spot—whether it's a coastal walk like Bondi to Coogee or a quiet hideaway in the Royal National Park—is surprisingly difficult.
> 
> Today, I am excited to present **SCAPE**, a beautiful, experience-driven web application designed to help students, tourists, and residents effortlessly rediscover nature and share their outdoor journeys. Let's explore how SCAPE redefines outdoor discovery."

### Delivery Tips:
* **Tone:** Start with a calm, grounded, and engaging tone. 
* **Pacing:** Speak slowly during the introduction to establish a serene, nature-focused atmosphere that matches the branding.
* **Emphasis:** Highlight the contrast between the "busy city life" and the "need to connect with nature."

## Slide 2: The Urban Exploration Dilemma
* **Slide Title:** The Urban Exploration Dilemma (Problem Statement)
* **Visuals:** Dual-card layout in light sage green. The left card outlines core problems; the right card details the real-world impact and targeted struggles of students and tourists.
* **Duration:** 1.5 minutes

### Presenter Script:
> "Let's look at the core problem we are solving. If you want to go on a hike or find a quiet picnic spot in Sydney, where do you look? 
> 
> Right now, outdoor information is scattered all over the place—split between unstructured travel blogs, random social media posts, and traditional navigation apps. But here's the catch: standard mapping apps are designed for *driving navigation (A-to-B)*, not for *experiencing nature*. They don't tell you the difficulty of a trail, the fitness required, the surrounding facilities, or whether a spot is good for relaxing or reading a book.
> 
> For students, tourists, and new residents, this leads to ineffective planning, wasted hours, and missed opportunities. They either end up in overcrowded spots or simply stay indoors because planning feels too overwhelming. That's a major missed connection to Sydney's gorgeous natural backyard."

### Delivery Tips:
* **Tone:** Empathetic and analytical. Use a slightly disappointed tone when describing the limitations of traditional maps.
* **Body Language:** Nod in agreement with the struggles, connecting with the audience's potential experiences of getting lost or feeling overwhelmed.

## Slide 9: Tech Stack & System Architecture
* **Slide Title:** Tech Stack & Architecture
* **Visuals:** Three vertical columns mapping: Frontend Layer, Mapping & API Layer, and Backend & Database Layer.
* **Duration:** 1.5 minutes

### Presenter Script:
> "Now let's talk about what makes SCAPE run under the hood. We chose a modern, scalable, and highly robust tech stack.
> 
> On the **Frontend**, we use **Next.js with React and TypeScript**. This gives us strong type-safety and swift page rendering. Styling is done completely in **Vanilla CSS** to give us maximum layout flexibility and ensure that every single glassmorphism blur and nature theme element is custom-crafted.
> 
> In the **Mapping Layer**, we integrated **Maplibre GL** combined with OpenStreetMap and Mapbox layers, managing map data via lightweight GeoJSON files to keep performance incredibly fast.
> 
> On the **Backend and Database**, we rely on **Node.js Express** and Next.js serverless API routes. Our database is managed via the **Prisma ORM**, connecting to a local SQL/SQLite database for rapid development. Authentication is fully secured using hashed passwords and JSON Web Token (JWT) session cookies."

### Delivery Tips:
* **Tone:** Professional, technical, and assured.
* **Emphasis:** Reassure the audience that the stack is highly robust, scalable, and secure.

## Presentation Q&A Preparation Notes
Here are answers to potential questions evaluators might ask about the project:

1. **Why choose Vanilla CSS instead of Tailwind?**
   * *Answer:* "Vanilla CSS allowed us to create custom, reusable glassmorphism tokens and delicate nature-themed variables without bloating our HTML classes or fighting framework constraints. It gives us precise control over animations and backdrop filters."
2. **Why Maplibre GL over Google Maps?**
   * *Answer:* "Maplibre GL is open-source, highly performant with vector tiles, and provides a clean API for custom-styled coordinate pins. It is cost-effective for a community-focused platform and gives us full visual control over map styling."
3. **How is data persisted?**
   * *Answer:* "We use Prisma ORM to interact with our SQLite/MySQL database, which holds user records, favorite spot lists, and nature blog posts."
